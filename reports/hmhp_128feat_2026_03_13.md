## 6. Key Metrics for 0DTE Trading Strategy

### 6.1 Directional Accuracy (most relevant metric)

The model achieves **63.8% directional accuracy** (excluding "Stable" predictions) on both exchanges. When the model predicts Up or Down, it is correct 63.8% of the time. This directly maps to the 0DTE strategy: enter ATM options only on directional signals.

### 6.2 Signal Rate

The model produces directional signals (non-Stable predictions) **71-72%** of the time on XNAS and **69-70%** on ARCX. The remaining 28-31% are "Stable" predictions where the model abstains — these are the uncertain periods where the 0DTE strategy should not trade.

### 6.3 Agreement Ratio as Confidence Filter

When all 3 horizons agree (agreement_ratio ≈ 1.0), the prediction is more reliable. The 91-92% agreement ratio means most predictions have cross-horizon confirmation. The strategy should filter for high agreement: trade only when H10, H60, and H300 all point in the same direction.

### 6.4 Confirmation Score

The confirmation score (0.60-0.61) measures the model's self-assessed confidence after cross-horizon integration. Higher values indicate stronger conviction. Filtering for confirmation_score > 0.7 should yield a subset of trades with higher win rate at the cost of fewer signals.

## 7. Confidence-Conditioned Analysis

This section quantifies the "high conviction" signal quality by computing accuracy conditioned on the model's self-assessed confidence metrics. This directly determines the 0DTE strategy's trade selection criteria.

### 7.1 Confidence Metric Definitions

- **agreement_ratio**: Fraction of horizons predicting the same class as the majority vote. With 3 horizons, values are 1.0 (all agree), 0.667 (2/3 agree), or 0.333 (all different).
- **confirmation_score**: `0.5 × agreement_ratio + 0.5 × avg_decoder_confidence`. Upper bound is ~0.667 because decoder confidence per-class saturates near 0.333 (calibrated uniform across 3 classes).

### 7.2 Agreement Ratio Distribution

| Agreement Level | XNAS Test | ARCX Test |
|---|---|---|
| 1.0 (all 3 agree) | 38,816 (76.5%) | 14,181 (76.7%) |
| 0.667 (2/3 agree) | 11,333 (22.3%) | 4,175 (22.6%) |
| 0.333 (all different) | 575 (1.1%) | 135 (0.7%) |

The distribution is remarkably stable across exchanges: ~76-77% full agreement, ~22-23% partial, ~1% total disagreement.

### 7.3 Confirmation Score Distribution

| Percentile | XNAS Test | ARCX Test |
|---|---|---|
| p10 | 0.4793 | 0.4643 |
| p25 | 0.6307 | 0.6115 |
| p50 | 0.6505 | 0.6370 |
| p75 | 0.6595 | 0.6490 |
| p90 | 0.6646 | 0.6580 |
| p99 | 0.6666 | 0.6648 |

**Architectural ceiling**: The confirmation_score cannot exceed ~0.667 because it is `0.5 × agreement + 0.5 × avg_confidence`, and decoder confidence per-class is naturally bounded near 1/num_classes = 0.333 for a well-calibrated 3-class model. The practical "high confidence" threshold is **0.65**, not 0.70.

### 7.4 Accuracy by Confidence Tier (Test Set)

| Confidence Tier | XNAS N | XNAS Acc | XNAS DirAcc | ARCX N | ARCX Acc | ARCX DirAcc |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| All samples | 50,724 | 59.63% | 88.55% | 18,491 | 58.82% | 88.40% |
| **All horizons agree** (=1.0) | 38,816 (76.5%) | **63.08%** | **89.52%** | 14,181 (76.7%) | **62.20%** | **89.03%** |
| **High conviction** (agree=1.0 AND confirm>0.65) | 26,142 (51.5%) | **68.72%** | **93.88%** | 4,314 (23.3%) | **78.63%** | **97.21%** |
| Horizons disagree (<1.0) | 11,908 (23.5%) | 48.38% | 80.59% | 4,310 (23.3%) | 47.70% | 82.32% |

### 7.5 Key Findings

**Finding 1: Agreement filter adds +3.4pp to accuracy.**
When all 3 horizons (H10, H60, H300) predict the same direction, accuracy rises from ~59% to ~63% on both exchanges. This filter retains ~76% of all predictions — a mild selectivity with meaningful accuracy gain.

**Finding 2: High-conviction filter reaches 68.7% (XNAS) and 78.6% (ARCX) accuracy.**
Adding the decoder confidence requirement (`confirm>0.65`) further filters to samples where the model is genuinely confident. On XNAS this retains 51.5% of samples at 68.7% accuracy (+9.1pp). On ARCX the filter is much more selective (23.3%) but yields 78.6% accuracy (+19.8pp).

**Finding 3: Directional accuracy at high conviction is 93.9% (XNAS) and 97.2% (ARCX).**
When the model predicts a directional move (Up or Down) with full cross-horizon agreement and high decoder confidence, it identifies the correct direction 93.9–97.2% of the time. For 0DTE ATM options where direction is what matters, this is the actionable signal.

**Finding 4: ARCX produces sharper confidence separation.**
ARCX's thinner order book creates more binary signals: the model is either very confident (23.3% at 78.6%) or uncertain. XNAS's deeper book produces more moderate confidence levels (51.5% at 68.7%). This suggests ARCX may be the preferred venue for high-conviction 0DTE signal generation, despite having lower overall accuracy.

**Finding 5: Disagreement confirms uncertainty.**
When horizons disagree (agreement < 1.0), accuracy drops to 47.7–48.4% — near random for a 3-class problem (33% baseline). This validates that the agreement ratio is a genuine uncertainty indicator, not noise. **The strategy should never trade when horizons disagree.**

### 7.6 Implications for 0DTE Trading Strategy

| Strategy Parameter | Recommended Value | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Trade filter | agreement_ratio = 1.0 | Eliminates 23% of low-quality signals |
| High-conviction filter | confirmation_score > 0.65 | Achieves 69-79% accuracy, 94-97% directional |
| Never trade when | agreement < 1.0 | 48% accuracy = no edge |
| Preferred signal source | ARCX for high-conviction | Sharper confidence, 97% directional accuracy |
| Expected trade rate | 23-52% of all predictions | Depends on conviction threshold |

## 8. Conclusions

1. **HMHP beats all benchmarks:** 59.62% H10 on XNAS (vs 58.2% prior, 38% XGBoost), confirming temporal modeling adds ~20pp over single-snapshot and MLOFI adds ~1.4pp over 116 features.

2. **Cross-exchange generalization validates signals:** Near-identical performance on XNAS and ARCX confirms the learned patterns are genuine microstructure properties of NVDA, not venue-specific artifacts.

3. **Minimal overfitting:** Train-val gap of 1.1pp after 30 epochs with 170K parameters on 163 training days indicates the architecture is well-regularized.

4. **H10 is the actionable horizon:** The ~20pp gap between H10 (59.6%) and H60 (41.2%) confirms that signal strength decays rapidly. For 0DTE trading, the model should be used for very short-term predictions (10 events ≈ 1 second).

5. **Confidence filtering unlocks 68-79% accuracy and 94-97% directional accuracy.** The confidence-conditioned analysis (Section 7) reveals that filtering for full cross-horizon agreement plus high decoder confidence yields a high-conviction subset where the model correctly identifies the direction of the next move 93.9% (XNAS) to 97.2% (ARCX) of the time. This is the primary signal for the 0DTE strategy.

6. **ARCX produces the sharpest high-conviction signals.** Despite lower overall accuracy (58.8% vs 59.6%), ARCX's high-conviction subset achieves 78.6% accuracy and 97.2% directional accuracy — the strongest signal anywhere in our pipeline. The thinner book creates more decisive microstructure patterns.

7. **Disagreement = no edge.** When horizons disagree, accuracy drops to ~48% (near random). The agreement ratio is a reliable uncertainty indicator and should be used as a hard gate for trade entry.
